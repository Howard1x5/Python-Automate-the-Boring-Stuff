# Chapter 4: Lists - Summary

- **Lists are ordered collections of values**—basically, a more powerful version of variables.
- Lists use **square brackets** (`[]`) and store **multiple values** inside.
- **Access elements with indexes** (`myList[0]` gets the first item).
- Lists can store **any data type**—strings, numbers, even other lists.
- **Slicing (`myList[start:end]`)** lets you grab a portion of a list.
- **Lists are mutable**—meaning I can change their values directly.
- **Appending vs. Inserting vs. Removing:**
  - `.append(value)` adds to the **end**.
  - `.insert(index, value)` adds **at a specific position**.
  - `.remove(value)` deletes **by value** (not index).
- **Looping through a list (`for item in myList`)** makes iterating easy.
- **List methods** like `.sort()`, `.reverse()`, and `.index()` help manipulate lists quickly.
- **The Magic 8 Ball exercise** reinforced **random choices in a list**.
- **The Coin Flip Streaks exercise** showed how to **count patterns in a list** (important for automation).
- **The Character Picture Grid exercise** forced me to think differently—using a **nested loop to print column-first instead of row-first**.
